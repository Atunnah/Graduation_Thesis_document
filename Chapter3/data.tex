\section{Data Preparation and Preprocessing}
\label{sec:data_preparation_and_preprocessing}
The absence of sufficiently large, annotated Vietnamese OCR datasets, as established in Section \ref{sec:existing_vnese_ocr_datasets_and_benchmark}, necessitates a fully synthetic data generation strategy for both stages of the cascaded pipeline. Two independent generation pipelines are developed: one producing scene-level document images with oriented bounding box annotations for training the RT-DETR detection stage, and one producing isolated text line images with transcription labels for training the PARSeq recognition stage. Together, these pipelines yield approximately 130,000 annotated detection images and 2,000,000 recognition line crops, constituting the complete training corpus for this work. Neither pipeline requires manual annotation effort, as all ground truth labels are derived directly from the rendering parameters used during generation.

\subsection{Detection Dataset}
The detection training corpus is generated by a procedural document image generator that renders Vietnamese text onto synthetic backgrounds. It computes exact oriented bounding box (OBB) annotations directly from the rendering parameters and exports the labels in the YOLO OBB format. Each annotation record encodes a single text line as a five-parameter tuple $(c_x, c_y, w, h, \theta)$ normalized by the image dimensions, where $\theta \in [0^\circ, 360^\circ)$ denotes the counter-clockwise rotation angle of the text line relative to the horizontal axis.

\subsubsection{Corpus and Font Collection}
Text content is sampled from a curated Vietnamese corpus comprising news articles, administrative documents, receipt templates, and general prose, providing lexical and typographic diversity across formal and informal registers. A collection of Vietnamese-compatible TrueType and OpenType fonts is assembled, each accompanied by a character set manifest file that enumerates the Unicode code points the font supports. At generation time, each text string is matched only to fonts whose character set fully covers the required Vietnamese composite characters, preventing rendering failures on diacritically complex strings. Font sizes are sampled uniformly from a range spanning 10 to 96 pixels depending on the document layout type, producing text line heights that span the full range encountered in real administrative documents

\subsubsection{Document Layout Types}
Each synthetic image is assigned one of seven layout archetypes, as shown in Figure \ref{fig:data_illustration}, sampled according to a fixed distribution that reflects the diversity of Vietnamese document formats encountered in practice. Single-column layouts simulate standard administrative correspondence and printed forms. Wide-format layouts simulate landscape-oriented tables and spreadsheet exports. Small-card layouts simulate identity card crops and business card formats. Dense-text layouts simulate newspaper columns and legal document pages. Multi-column layouts, including two-column and three-column variants, simulate academic publications and government gazettes. Receipt layouts simulate retail point-of-sale printouts with characteristic formatting elements including store headers, product line items, subtotal rows, barcodes, and separator lines. Rotated-scatter layouts place text lines at arbitrary orientations across the canvas with no alignment constraints, providing training signal for the detector's angle regression head across the full $[0^\circ, 360^\circ)$ range.

Image canvas dimensions are sampled per layout type from predefined width and height ranges. Background colors are sampled from near-white ranges with randomized RGB values, and text colors are sampled from near-black ranges, producing the high-contrast monochrome appearance typical of printed documents while introducing controlled color variation. The six layout archetypes are sampled according to a fixed probability distribution 
rather than uniformly, with single-column and dense-text layouts assigned higher sampling weights to reflect their prevalence in real Vietnamese administrative and legal documents. Receipt and rotated-scatter layouts are assigned lower weights, as they represent less common document categories but are nonetheless critical for ensuring coverage of challenging formatting patterns and arbitrary text orientations in the training corpus.

\subsubsection{Angle Distribution and the Scatter Patch}
An initial analysis of the base generator revealed that the use of \texttt{cv2.minAreaRect} to fit bounding boxes around rendered text caused the reported angles to collapse toward a small set of values near $0^\circ$, $90^\circ$, and $270^\circ$, regardless of the true rendering angle. This occurred because \texttt{minAreaRect} fits the minimum-area enclosing rectangle and always reports angles in $[-90^\circ, 0^\circ]$, introducing systematic quantization that would bias the angle regression head toward a degenerate distribution.

To correct this, a targeted patch replaces the bounding box computation with a direct PIL-based rendering pipeline that retains the original randomly sampled angle exactly. Text is rendered onto a transparent mask, the mask is rotated by the sampled angle using PIL's \texttt{rotate} with \texttt{expand=True}, and the oriented bounding box is computed analytically from the original text dimensions and rotation angle without any re-fitting step. Simultaneously, the spatial layout strategy is overridden to force $100\%$ scatter placement, ensuring that text line centers are distributed uniformly across the image canvas rather than concentrated in document-typical regions. This dual correction produces a training set with a flat angle distribution across the full $[0^\circ, 360^\circ)$ range and uniform spatial coverage, both of which are necessary for the RT-DETR angle head to generalize to arbitrary document orientations.

The YOLO OBB label format adopted stores each annotation as:
\begin{verbatim}
0  cx_norm  cy_norm  w_norm  h_norm  angle_deg
\end{verbatim}
where all spatial coordinates are normalized to $[0, 1]$ by the image dimensions and the angle is stored as a float with two decimal places of precision to avoid quantization loss.

\subsubsection{Negative Samples}
To reduce the false positive rate of the detector on non-text regions, a set of negative training images is generated by randomly cropping sub-regions from a collection of natural and document background images that contain no text. These crops are produced at variable dimensions spanning the height and width ranges of real text line detections, and are added to the training set with empty annotation files, teaching the detector to suppress activations on textureless backgrounds, graphical elements, and decorative patterns.

\subsubsection{Image-level Augmentation for Detection}
After text rendering and annotation, each synthetic image undergoes a stochastic augmentation pipeline applied at three intensity levels, namely normal, medium, and heavy, sampled uniformly per image. Augmentations include Gaussian blur with variable kernel size to simulate lens defocus and scan softening, additive Gaussian noise to simulate sensor noise and JPEG compression artifacts, brightness and contrast jitter through PIL's \texttt{ImageEnhance}, random horizontal flip with corresponding angle correction, random rotation of the full canvas with bounding box coordinate transformation, and perspective warp through random displacement of the four image corners via the OpenCV function \texttt{cv2.getPerspectiveTransform}. The OBB-aware rotation augmentation correctly updates both the center coordinates and the rotation angle of each bounding box under the full canvas rotation, ensuring that augmented annotations remain geometrically consistent.

The generation procedure for a single detection image is summarized in Algorithm~\ref{alg:detection_data_generation}. At each iteration, a layout archetype is selected by sampling a uniform random variable against the fixed probability thresholds, and the corresponding renderer produces the image together with its raw OBB annotations. The augmentation pipeline then operates on the rendered image and bounding boxes jointly, ensuring that all geometric transforms, namely canvas rotation and perspective warp, propagate analytically to the OBB parameters rather than re-fitting boxes from scratch. The final image is saved as JPEG and each bounding box is written to the label file in YOLO OBB format with coordinates normalized by the image dimensions. The complete detection dataset is publicly available \href{https://drive.google.com/drive/folders/1Q453JEqpMgMKteGG4cbb6Nl09tVWzLd3?usp=sharing}{\underline{here}}.

\begin{algorithm}[H]
\scriptsize
\caption{Data Generation Pipeline for a Single Detection Image}
\label{alg:detection_data_generation}
\begin{algorithmic}[1]
\Require Vietnamese text corpus $\mathcal{C}$, font collection $\mathcal{F}$ with charset manifests
\Ensure Generated image $img$ and YOLO OBB label file
\For{\textbf{each} image index $i$}
    \State $n \gets \text{sample from } [5, 60]$; \quad $\text{lines} \gets n \text{ consecutive strings from } \mathcal{C}$
    \State $aug \gets \text{sample level from } \{\text{normal} \times 3,\, \text{medium} \times 2,\, \text{heavy} \times 1\}$
    \State $r \gets \mathcal{U}[0, 1)$
    \If{$r < 0.10$} $img, bboxes \gets \textsc{RenderTopTwoBottomOne}(\text{lines}, aug)$
    \ElsIf{$r < 0.20$} $img, bboxes \gets \textsc{RenderTopOneBottomTwo}(\text{lines}, aug)$
    \ElsIf{$r < 0.30$} $img, bboxes \gets \textsc{RenderTwoColumn}(\text{lines}, aug)$
    \ElsIf{$r < 0.40$} $img, bboxes \gets \textsc{RenderThreeColumn}(\text{lines}, aug)$
    \ElsIf{$r < 0.53$} $img, bboxes \gets \textsc{RenderReceipt}(aug)$
    \ElsIf{$r < 0.68$} $img, bboxes \gets \textsc{RenderRotatedScatter}(\text{lines}, aug)$
    \Else
        \State $mrf \gets \text{sample from } \{0.0 \times 3,\, 0.05,\, 0.10\}$
        \State $layout \gets \text{sample from } \mathcal{L}$
        \Comment{five standard layout archetypes}
        \State $img, bboxes \gets \textsc{RenderDocument}(\text{lines}, layout, aug, mrf)$
    \EndIf
    \If{$img = \varnothing$ \textbf{or} $bboxes = \varnothing$} \textbf{continue} \EndIf
    \State \textbf{// Augmentation pipeline}
    \If{$aug \in \{\text{medium}, \text{heavy}\}$ \textbf{and} $\mathcal{U} < 0.6$}
        $img \gets \textsc{AddBackgroundTexture}(img)$
    \EndIf
    \If{$\mathcal{U} < p_{noise}(aug)$} $img \gets \textsc{AddGaussianNoise}(img)$ \EndIf
    \If{$\mathcal{U} < p_{blur}(aug)$} $img \gets \textsc{AddGaussianBlur}(img)$ \EndIf
    \If{$aug \in \{\text{medium}, \text{heavy}\}$} $img \gets \textsc{AdjustBrightnessContrast}(img)$ \EndIf
    \If{$\mathcal{U} < p_{rot}(aug)$}
        \State $img, bboxes \gets \textsc{CanvasRotation}(img, bboxes)$
        \Comment{OBB centers and angles updated analytically}
    \EndIf
    \If{$aug = \text{heavy}$ \textbf{and} $\mathcal{U} < 0.25$}
        \State $img, bboxes \gets \textsc{PerspectiveWarp}(img, bboxes)$
        \Comment{corners displaced via \texttt{cv2.getPerspectiveTransform}}
    \EndIf
    \State \textbf{// Serialize}
    \State $img.\text{save}(\text{JPEG, quality} \sim \mathcal{U}[85, 98])$
    \For{\textbf{each} $(c_x, c_y, w, h, \theta) \in bboxes$}
        \State $label.\text{write}(\texttt{0}\ \ c_x/W\ \ c_y/H\ \ w/W\ \ h/H\ \ \theta)$
        \Comment{$\theta \in [0^\circ, 360^\circ)$, CCW}
    \EndFor
\EndFor
\end{algorithmic}
\end{algorithm}

\vspace{4em}

The final detection training set comprises approximately 130,000 synthetic images with a 90/10 train-validation split, stratified to maintain balanced representation of all layout types across both splits.

\subsection{Recognition Dataset}
The recognition training corpus is produced by a separate three-branch parallel generation pipeline that renders individual Vietnamese text line images at fixed height with variable width, paired with their Unicode transcription strings. The pipeline is orchestrated to produce approximately 2,000,000 labeled text line crops in total, distributed across three generator variants that differ in their background treatment and augmentation profile.

\subsubsection{Corpus and Character Set Design}
Text strings are sampled from a large Vietnamese corpus covering news, administrative documents, and general prose. Each string is rendered using a randomly selected compatible font, with compatibility verified against the font's character set manifest to ensure that all Vietnamese composite characters including full tonal and diacritical combinations are correctly rendered. The full Vietnamese character set used as the PARSeq output vocabulary is constructed to include all unique composite characters appearing in the corpus, covering all combinations of the seventeen modified vowel forms with the six tonal markers, in addition to digits, punctuation, and Latin characters. This atomic character set design, in which each composite glyph is treated as a single vocabulary token, avoids the diacritic decomposition problem discussed in Section \ref{sec:diacritical_marks_and_tonal_complexity}.

\subsubsection{Three-branch Generation Strategy}
The generation pipeline distributes the 2,000,000 target images across three specialized generators executed in parallel as independent subprocesses, with representative samples shown in Figure \ref{fig:recog_data_samples}. The dataset is sampled according to a fixed distribution across these branches to ensure optimal training diversity. The dominant clean branch (\texttt{gen\_normal\_v2}), comprising 60\% of the data, renders black text on near-white or lightly textured synthetic backgrounds, applying Gaussian blur, additive noise, and random brightness jitter to simulate printed document scan conditions. This branch constitutes the largest proportion of the training set because administrative document text lines predominantly appear in clean, high-contrast conditions. The augmented branch (\texttt{gen\_aug\_v2}), making up 20\% of the data, renders text against real photographic backgrounds cropped from a collection of natural scene images to simulate complex background textures. This branch additionally applies 3D perspective rotation using a camera projection model, cylindrical warp to simulate page curvature, shear-based italic simulation, and mixed-font rendering. These transformations substantially increase the geometric and photometric diversity of the training distribution. Finally, the mixed branch (\texttt{gen\_normal\_aug\_v2}), comprising the remaining 20\%, combines pastel-toned synthetic backgrounds with augmented color schemes, sampling both text and background colors from broader ranges to produce training examples with moderate color variation that bridges the two extremes.

\subsubsection{Text Line Rendering and Preprocessing}
Each text line image is rendered at a target height of 48 pixels, with width determined by the rendered text extent plus a small symmetric padding margin. Aspect ratio is preserved throughout. The rendered image is saved as JPEG with quality sampled uniformly between 85 and 98, introducing mild compression artifact variation that improves recognizer robustness to low-quality document scans.

At training time, each recognition image is individually preprocessed by an aspect-ratio-preserving resize to $48 \times 1024$ pixels, followed by symmetric zero-padding on the width dimension if the rendered width is shorter than 1024 pixels. Pixel values are normalized using ImageNet mean $(0.485, 0.456, 0.406)$ and per-channel standard deviation $(0.229, 0.224, 0.225)$, matching the pretraining statistics of the PARSeq ViT backbone. This preprocessing is applied identically during training and inference to ensure consistent input statistics.

The generation pseudocode for a single recognition image is summarized as follows:
\begin{algorithm}[ht]
\caption{Data Generation Pipeline for a Single Recognition Image}
\label{alg:data_generation}
\begin{algorithmic}[1]
\Require Text string corpus $\mathcal{C}$
\Ensure Generated image $img$ and label file
\For{\textbf{each} text string $s$ \textbf{from} $\mathcal{C}$}
    \State $font \gets \text{select compatible font for } s$
    \State $size \gets \text{sample from } [10, 96]$
    \State $branch \gets \text{sample branch (60\% normal, 20\% aug, 20\% mixed)}$
    \State $img \gets \text{render}(s, font, size, \text{background}[branch])$
    \State $img \gets \text{apply\_augmentation}(img, branch)$
    \State $img \gets \text{tight\_crop}(img, \text{margin}=2\text{--}5\text{px})$
    \State $img.\text{save}(\text{JPEG, quality} \sim \mathcal{U}[85, 98])$
    \State $label.\text{write}(s)$
\EndFor
\end{algorithmic}
\end{algorithm}

\subsubsection{Dataset Statistics and Split}
The complete recognition training corpus comprises approximately 2,000,000 labeled text line images with a 90/10 train-validation split. The vocabulary covers 232 unique Vietnamese composite characters in addition to digits, punctuation, and Latin characters, for a total character set size of approximately 310 tokens including the special begin-of-sequence, end-of-sequence, and padding tokens required by the PARSeq tokenizer. The median text line length is 12 characters, with the distribution spanning from 1 to 25 characters, matching the maximum label length configured in the PARSeq model. The complete recognition dataset is publicly available \href{https://drive.google.com/drive/folders/1k3GEfYtW_D61z3JAUfHinRIHWLU2HdkD?usp=sharing}{\underline{here}}.
