\chapter*{Abstract}
\addcontentsline{toc}{chapter}{Abstract}
The digitization of Vietnamese documents has become an urgent priority for administrative agencies and enterprises. Optical character recognition (OCR) serves as the key enabling technology, yet Vietnamese orthographic complexity poses substantial challenges, including a vast character inventory, minimal visual distinctions between diacritically similar characters, and diverse layouts with arbitrary text orientations.

This thesis proposes a cascaded deep learning architecture of two independently optimized stages. The first employs a fine-tuned RT-DETR V2 detector with an auxiliary angle regression head using sine-cosine parameterization and Gaussian Wasserstein Distance loss. The second uses a fine-tuned PARSeq Vision Transformer adapted to Vietnamese through vocabulary extension to approximately 310 composite tokens, class-weighted cross-entropy loss, and a phonological validity mask at decoding. A perspective-corrected inter-stage interface converts oriented text regions into upright crops, linking angular localization quality to diacritical recognition accuracy.

To address data scarcity, two independent synthetic generation pipelines are developed: a detection pipeline producing approximately 130,000 annotated document images across seven layout archetypes with full $[0^\circ, 360^\circ)$ angular coverage, and a recognition pipeline producing approximately 2,000,000 labeled crops across clean printed, augmented scene text, and mixed-background conditions.

Experiments show the system substantially outperforms established baselines. RT-DETR achieves F1@0.50 of 0.501 and mAP@0.5:0.95 of 0.147, versus 0.134 and 0.008 for EAST. PARSeq achieves a Character Error Rate of 10.18\% and Word Error Rate of 34.71\%, outperforming VietOCR, PaddleOCR, Tesseract, and EasyOCR. The system is deployed as a modular pipeline with component encapsulation facilitating future upgrades without full retraining.
